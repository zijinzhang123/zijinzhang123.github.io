I received a PhD in Technology & Operations from the Stephen M. Ross School of Business at the University of Michigan, where I was fortunate to be advised by Professor Hyun-Soo Ahn and Professor Lennart Baardman. Prior to that, I earned a bachelor's degree in Mathematics and Statistics from Nanjing University and studied as an exchange student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison in 2017.

I will be joining the Carroll School of Management at Boston College as an Assistant Professor of Business Analytics in Fall 2025.

My research goal is to improve data-driven decisions in imperfect environments where operational misalignments exist or obtaining perfect data is impossible. By developing models grounded in practice, my research provides algorithmic solutions and novel insights for retail analytics, inventory management, and emerging societal challenges. Methodologically, I employ tools such as mathematical modeling, dynamic programming, stochastic optimization, and probability analysis.

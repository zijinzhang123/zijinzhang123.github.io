Welcome! I am Zijin (pronounced ZEE-jin). I received my Ph.D. in Technology & Operations from the Stephen M. Ross School of Business at the University of Michigan, where I was fortunate to be advised by Professor Hyun-Soo Ahn and Professor Lennart Baardman. Prior to that, I earned a B.S. degree in Mathematics and Statistics from Nanjing University and studied as an exchange student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

I will be joining the Carroll School of Management at Boston College as an Assistant Professor of Business Analytics in Fall 2025.

My research explores the engineering, economic, and social value of data through the lens of operational decision-making, with applications in retail analytics, nonprofit operations, and emerging AI markets. I focus on three core questions:

  1. (1) How data quality and bias shape decisions — learning with biased information;
  2. (2) What level of data precision or scale is “good enough” — guided by data design and active learning;
  3. (3) Where, how, and from whom data should be acquired — raising issues of data ethics and regulation.

Methodologically, I employ tools such as mathematical modeling, dynamic programming, stochastic optimization, and probability analysis.

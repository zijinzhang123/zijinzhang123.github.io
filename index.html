Welcome! I am Zijin (pronounced ZEE-jin). I received my Ph.D. in Technology & Operations from the Stephen M. Ross School of Business at the University of Michigan, where I was fortunate to be advised by Professor Hyun-Soo Ahn and Professor Lennart Baardman. Prior to that, I earned a B.S. degree in Mathematics and Statistics from Nanjing University and studied as an exchange student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

I will be joining the Carroll School of Management at Boston College as an Assistant Professor of Business Analytics in Fall 2025.

My research explores how data can be strategically managed and applied to improve decision-making in the context of retail analytics, nonprofit operations, and emerging AI-driven markets. I address three core dimensions of data value:

  • Engineering value: How to design data-driven algorithms that support real-time, large-scale decision-making under uncertainty.
  • Economic value: How to balance the cost of data acquisition with its decision impact via smart data selection and active learning.
  • Social value: How data practices impact society, fairness, and well-being, and shape policies on data ethics and technology regulation.

Methodologically, I employ tools from optimization, probability, game theory, and machine learning techniques.
